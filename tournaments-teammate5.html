Personal information

The name is Nguyễn Khôi Nguyên; I usually go by Maple so it is easier for people to call me. Although I am starting my 20s, I’m currently a freshman at RMIT, s3884511, with a quarter of the Chinese blood. I would play all kinds of games in my free time, ranging from gambling to outdoor activities, and I am a Tarot reader that does not foretell the future, but rather provide guidance. I am also an advocate of Japanese culture, particularly their fashion across the centuries. I picked up IT when I joined my secondary school’s programming team, and I have been interested since. Despite the fact that I am not very well-versed in the area, I still intend to pursue it until I become an experienced game developer. Though my knowledge about IT is still shallow, I have managed to attain a foundation for web development.

Career Plans

Like Assignment 1 and Assignment 2, in Assignment 3 my selection remains unchanged. I want to be a game developer, and in the distant future, I want to have a personal game studio. Looking at the planner boards, I can see that everyone on our team needs to learn how to code and develop apps for our purpose, though everyone has their choices for a different future such as a game developer, game programmer, programmer, or network security specialist. We have identified the path we need to take and are clearly aware of what we must overcome, what we must face. However, at this moment our goal is to create the perfect Magnum Opus.

The Thing that Assist My In My Career Plan

The entirety of Magnum Opus is my career, as I have mentioned about taking on this project even after the course is finished. It is also because this is within my field of knowledge so I have already prepared beforehand the fundamentals necessary to build a card game, and even more so, the course’s basic requirement of web development. However, as Magnum Opus needs to be digital, I have yet to prepare myself with the required programming language C#/C++. Despite that, much of the progress of this project will aid me to further perfect the game, making Magnum Opus the finest gem in the digital card game industry. On the other hand, I also learned how to cooperate better with teammates, as opposed to my passive nature. This project has done more for me than I could have expected.

Feedback From Assignment 1 And 2

When the evaluation of my personal profile was released, I knew right away that I was once again lost in thoughts, and I could not make up my mind to which path I would follow. But as of now, I am confident that IT will be the future that I can foresee with great outcomes. Although it will also come with great responsibility, I know that it will be the best lesson to forge myself into a finer man, with a clear vision ahead of him and a dream of opening a game company that produces magnificent works.

Feedback For Group

A quick glance at our group shows a merry and relaxed yet dedicated and focused attitude. Although we may show up late at the appointed meetings, we always get the problem solved and get the job done. Following this is more detailed feedback for each member, including myself.

I’ll start with myself first. I judge myself as an extremely lazy person. I’m easy to be late on delivering the required work on time, and it has more or less created a little trouble for my teammates. Although I am the one who proposed the project and got approved, I still cannot make myself to be a responsible captain. However, I am always on the job to smooth the job of others by simplifying their process, allowing them to focus more on the grand tasks.

Next, I would like to talk about Như. She is the real acting leader who calls for meetings and setting deadlines, so everyone can get a grasp of the situation and get their job done in time. She was not well-versed in the subject of games, but I was able to communicate with her with ease as she is a quick learner. Her performance in the report is excellent despite the fact that she lacks the words needed to convey her messages.

Đại, the designer, is the next one on the line. He and I share the work of designing the game’s board, and he takes his work very seriously as he himself is an educated designer. Although he usually kids around, I can rest assured that he can deliver far better than what we proposed. He even intends to work with me on Magnum Opus after the course is over.

Thirdly, it is Quang that I want to mention. He was of great help when it comes to building the game rules since he is the only one in the group with certain experiences with board games. In return, I also help him with building Magnum Opus’ website. During the course of seeing him work, he put his trust in me when I confirmed with him that the website might look intimidating, but it was all appearance. The result is as you can see, a complete overhaul of the website’s template to make it our own.

To close up, we have Đức. In my opinion, he is your typical run-of-the-mill team member. I admire his efficacy in writing the report, but that is as far I can tell from the past weeks.
