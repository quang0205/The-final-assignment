Risks

In every project there are always risks involved, and as such preparing for the factors that can be hindrance for the group’s progress is of importance. Realistically there is no way to fully determine everything that could happen throughout the entirety of the project’s duration, so I only cover some situations that our group has a high chance of encountering. These risks can be classified into two groups: basic and advanced risks. Basic risks are risks are the somewhat mundane risks that someone will quickly point out in a project. Among those are risks regarding the tools and risks regarding the performance of human resources.

For software risks, since our project is about making a video game, we will most certainly be using Unity as the main platform for game developing with C#/C++ as the main programming languages, Adobe Illustrator for creating illustrations for the different components of the game, and Cloud technology for storing servers. In addition, we will also be using HTML, CSS and JavaScript to create the website for our game. This composition of apps and languages, although giving us full coverage of the software we need for the project, raise a lot of problems. First is that as of writing this report right now, none of us have any training or experience with using Unity or have training about C#/C++. To combat this problem, a Unity course should be taken at the same time as research on Unity documents and tutorials. Thankfully, documents and tutorials on the use of Unity is all over the internet. But this can also create unwanted situations. One of them is that according to my acquaintances who have experience in the IT field, Unity is a very hard system to learn properly and that it will take a long time, maybe much longer than we expect, and even if finished learning, certain features might still be lacking or ineffectively used. And as such, a long duration of time should be allocated to the learning of Unity, preferably during the upcoming term break, as it is also close to the Lunar New Year, the break’s duration will be extended to other timeframes. As for C#/C++, there should be courses that cover their usage from the degree’s curriculum, and it will be more than enough for our needs. As for the website, although we already have our introduction to web programming languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, our current understanding of them is nowhere enough to create a usable web out of them. That’s why for the upcoming assignment, we will just use free templates online and web design app such as Nicepage to generate the code for now. In the upcoming semesters there are courses detailing the method of web programming. We will use it as a basis to code and enhance our website from then on.

For basic human risks, there are two things that come up to mind: health and the member’s capability to follow the deadlines. These two risks can be intertwined to each other and in certain situations, one can be the cause of the other. Health issues can be divided into two parts: physical and mental health issues. Physical health issues for this kind of project should be quite mundane as someone is sick close to the deadline or having accidents that render them unable to continue working. Based on Vietnam’s current situation then the most likely of these are either from traffic accidents or the member is robbed and injured in the process. For these kinds of problems, the procedure should be similar, with changes to suit the specific situation but generally it should be that the current member’s work will either be divided among the rest or another person will take over depending on the type of work and the amount of time left until the deadline. Mental health issues for this project should be the accumulation of stress over a long period of time. This could either reduce the member’s productivity or in more severe cases could lead to breakdown and/or physical issues. For these kinds of problems, if the workload is becoming too much or the term break is looming on the horizon, then the most logical and efficient solution is to just suck it up and solder on until the semester or that specific wave of deadline ends. The break is right there and there is no reason to have a breakdown at that critical of a moment. If the problem shows up at early stages for currently in the mid-term, it is recommended that the member in question go to the RMIT Wellbeing department for possible therapy.

With advanced issues, the thing that comes up our mind is the issue of licenses, especially if it is for the software that we will use. Beside some software that allow free version such as Nicepage or Visual studio code, the rest require a license to access to its functions or to have access to the software itself. Especially hard for us is that the license for Adobe apps is required to gain access to the software itself, and it is too expensive for students to afford. A Cracked or pirated version might be a solution, but using it will surely create legal issues later on if we ever want to release our work.
